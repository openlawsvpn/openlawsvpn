# Contributors

All contributors to openlawsvpn must agree to the Contributor License
Agreement (CLA) before their contributions can be accepted.

## Why a CLA is required

openlawsvpn is dual-licensed: the community edition under LGPL-2.1-or-later,
and a commercial edition available under a separate commercial license.
To offer both, the project maintainers must hold sublicensing rights over
all contributions. The CLA grants these rights while you retain copyright
ownership of your own code.

## CLA Terms (Individual)

By submitting a pull request or patch to this project, you agree:

1. You have the right to grant this license (the code is your original work
   or you have permission from the owner).

2. You grant the openlawsvpn project maintainers a perpetual, worldwide,
   non-exclusive, royalty-free, irrevocable license to:
   - Use, copy, modify, and distribute your contribution
   - Sublicense your contribution under any license, including commercial
     licenses, without restriction

3. Your contribution is submitted under the LGPL-2.1-or-later license as
   declared in the LICENSE file in this repository.

4. You retain copyright ownership of your contribution.

## How to agree

Add the following line to your pull request description or commit message:

    I agree to the openlawsvpn CLA as described in CONTRIBUTORS.md

## Corporate contributors

If you are contributing on behalf of your employer, a corporate CLA is
required. Contact the maintainers before submitting.
